# Kakuro Competitor Analysis

> Source: Gemini Deep Research (Jan 2026)

## Market Overview

The Kakuro market is bifurcated:
- **"Pure" Kakuro books** for enthusiasts
- **"Mixed" puzzle books** where Kakuro is part of a broader brain-training proposition

---

## Top Competitor Profiles

### Competitor A: The "Brain Health" Leader
**Title:** *The Ultimate Brain Health Puzzle Book for Adults*  
**Publisher:** Rockridge Press / Callisto

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| BSR | ~3,036 (Books Overall) |
| Est. Sales | ~2,000 copies/month |
| Est. Revenue | $3,200 - $10,000/month |
| Reviews | 4,575+ ratings (4.6/5 avg) |
| List Price | $12.99 (often $8.43) |
| Pages | 146 |
| Trim | 8.5" x 11" (Large Print) |
| Format | Paperback + Spiral-bound |

**Strategy:** Sells "Brain Health" not "Kakuro". Uses Kakuro as one of several tools (Sudoku, Cryptograms) to deliver cognitive longevity. Progression system (Warm-up to Difficult) encourages retention.

---

### Competitor B: The "Pure" Enthusiast Option
**Title:** *Brown Belt Kakuro* (Conceptis Puzzles)  
**Publisher:** Sterling Publishing / Conceptis

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Target | Intermediate to Advanced |
| Pages | 128 |
| Price | $5.95 - $9.95 |

**Strategy:** Uses martial arts ranking ("Brown Belt") to signify difficulty. Appeals to "mastery" psychology. Enables multiple SKUs (White Belt, Green Belt, Black Belt) from same content pipeline.

---

### Competitor C: The "Volume" Value Play
**Title:** *Giant Kakuro Puzzle Book*

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Grid Size | 20x20 (larger than standard) |
| Volume | 100 puzzles |
| Trim | 8.5" x 11" |

**Strategy:** Competes on "more for less". Giant grids target hardcore solvers who find standard 10x10 or 12x12 puzzles too quick.

## Review Sentiment Analysis

| Category | 5-Star Praises | 1-Star Complaints |
|----------|----------------|-------------------|
| **Usability** | "Large print is easy on the eyes", "Spiral binding lays flat" | "Numbers too small", "Spine makes writing hard" |
| **Difficulty** | "Good progression easy→hard", "Challenging but logical" | "Requires guessing", "Instructions unclear" |
| **Quality** | "Thick paper, no bleed-through" | "Spelling errors", "Multiple solutions" |
| **Content** | "Great variety", "Keeps brain sharp" | "Repetitive", "Computer-generated" |

### Critical Insight
> ⚠️ **Fatal Flaw:** The most damaging criticism is **multiple solutions** or **requiring guessing**. High-quality Kakuro must be solvable by logic alone. Your generator MUST guarantee unique solutions.

---

## Strategic Takeaways

1. **"Brain Health" positioning** outsells pure "Kakuro" branding
2. **Large Print** is a conversion driver for senior demographic
3. **Spiral-bound** option differentiates for serious solvers
4. **Series/Belt system** creates multiple SKUs from same pipeline
5. **Quality control** (unique solutions) is non-negotiable
